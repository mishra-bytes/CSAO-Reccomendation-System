# Why Our Synthetic Data Realistically Mimics Indian Food Delivery Behavior

## Modeled distributions
- City segmentation (Tier-1/Tier-2) via restaurant and order generation priors in data prep scripts.
- Meal-time behavior with hour buckets (breakfast/lunch/dinner/late-night) and downstream serving features.
- Cuisine + cart-completion behavior through category affinity, meal archetypes, and co-occurrence retrievers.
- Cart-size and basket realism through order-item sequence generation and session transitions.
- Veg/non-veg and price sensitivity represented in item metadata and ranking/item features.

## Evidence
- Reproducible aggregation and profiling are generated by `scripts/run_full_evaluation.py` and `scripts/run_offline_eval.py`.
- Segment-level behavior can be examined using `evaluation/segments/segment_analysis.py`.

## Limitations
- Synthetic datasets cannot perfectly capture local festival seasonality or micro-neighborhood tastes.
- Despite this, realism is sufficient for hackathon validation because evaluation includes ranking quality, diversity, latency, and cold-start robustness on consistent reproducible pipelines.
